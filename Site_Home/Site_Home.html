DISCOVER THE TYPES OF GUITAR

Classic Guitar

It is perhaps the best way to learn to play the guitar as a beginner.

Mainly because it is small, light and has nylon strings in general, making it more confortable.

The price is one of the attractives of this type of guitar.

It is common for the type of classical guitar to not be electric.

Folk Guitar

A little larger than the classic guitar in the body, but thinner in the fretboard.

Emits a louder and thinner sound than the previous one, making it a good option for playing with bands.

Not recomended for begginers because of your steel strings, which is harder than the nylon strings.

Accentuation of sound beauty in the mids and highs.

12 String Guitar

It has double stringing and tuning pegs.

The 4 lowest pairs of strings are tuned to the same note one octave higher.

The 2 highest doubles are tuned to the same octave.

This is one of the most interesting types of guitar for playing Blues, being one of the most beautiful sounds.

Flat Guitar

Made only in the electric version, giving you the option to equalize your sound.

Has a thin body, allowing slfter sounds. Can be found with Nylon or steel string.

Interesting option for those who want to play MPB.

BENEFITS OF PLAYING GUITAR

Cognition and Memory

By learning and memorising musical pieces, you strengthen your brain, improving memory and attention.

Motor Skills and Focus

Strumming and making chords improves motor coordination and focus, developing synchrony between hands and eyes, benefiting other areas of life that require precise motor control.

Stress and Well-Being

Playing guitar can be an effective way of managing stress. Focusing on playing helps relieve daily tension, functioning as an active meditation.

Discipline Improvement

Playing guitar requires discipline and dedication. Establishing a study routine helps develop self-discipline.

Creative Expression and Self-Expression

The guitar is a creative scape, allowing the expression of emotions and ideas. Composing and improvising can be a form of self-expression and communication.

Social interaction

The guitar opens doors to new social interactions and even performances. Joining groups or communities creates a sense of belonging and cultural sharing.

Cultural and Historical Development

Playing the guitar connects you with different cultures and traditions, exploring different genres and unique techniques.

Personal Satisfaction and Fulfillment

Playing the guitar provides a deep sense of accomplishment. Each song learned and piece composed increases self-confidence and self-esteem.

Healthy Aging

Research shows that playing an instrument helps keeping the brain active, which makes age-related diseases such as Alzheimer's more difficult.
